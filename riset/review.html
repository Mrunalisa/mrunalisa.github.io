by savita

An unassuming entry leading to a long and tricky climb up the steps of the hill and voila....you will reach the Pandavleni Caves, 13 of them, with carvings, and stone work. The climb can be even trickier during monsoon. Pretty much kept clean and maintained, as one would expect if 15rs per person (of indian origin, free for children) is charged an entree fee. From the top one can also see the extents of Nashik city and also get a good view of the highway leading to Mumbai. Date of experience: November 2019
